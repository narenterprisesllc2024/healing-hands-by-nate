# Five Bullet Friday — The Mowing Edition

*May 22*

Mowing season is here in earnest. If your low back has been talking to you since the weekend, these five are for you.

---

### 1. A move — the QL release that fixes "mowing back"

Lie on your back with a lacrosse ball (or tennis ball) tucked under the muscle between the top of your pelvis and your lowest rib, off to one side of your spine — **never on the spine itself**. Find a tender spot. Stay there. Breathe slowly through your nose. Don't roll around — the release happens when you settle in.

90 seconds per side, ideally the same evening as a long mow. It works because the quadratus lumborum is the muscle that locks up first when you've been bracing on the mower for a couple hours. Hit it before it sets.

### 2. A tool — a $5 lacrosse ball

The whole self-massage industry wants to sell you a $90 percussion gun. The truth is a $5 lacrosse ball does 80% of what those do for low back, glutes, feet, and shoulders. Tractor Supply in Union has them. So does any sporting goods store. Get two — one stays in the car, one stays by the bed.

If lacrosse-ball-firm is too much, start with a tennis ball. Move up when you're ready.

### 3. A nugget — Stuart McGill on the QL

If you've never heard of Stuart McGill, he's the Canadian spine researcher who wrote *Back Mechanic* — probably the best plain-language book on low back pain available. One of his core findings: the quadratus lumborum carries far more of the load in everyday stability than most people (or doctors) appreciate, and it's a muscle that responds beautifully to targeted release plus glute reactivation.

Translation: low back pain isn't always a disc thing. Often it's a QL-and-glute thing. The book's about $20 and worth every penny if you've been dealing with this for years.

### 4. A practice — 60 seconds of 4-7-8 breath

When your nervous system is parked in fight-or-flight, your muscles brace. The fastest way to drop the system is the 4-7-8 breath: inhale through your nose for 4, hold for 7, exhale through your mouth for 8. Four rounds takes about a minute.

Do this in the shower, in the car after a long day, or right before bed. It's the single most powerful 60 seconds in self-care, and it costs nothing.
