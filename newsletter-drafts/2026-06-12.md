# Five Bullet Friday — The Right Fit Edition

*June 12*

Summer's here. The humidity showed up this week, the evenings are long, and your body is running a different program than it was in April. Five things worth two minutes of your Friday.

---

### 1. A move — the figure-four stretch for summer driving hips

If you've been in the car more than usual — road trips, hauling kids to the pool, long drives to the lake — your piriformis is probably tight. It's a small muscle deep in your glute that clamps down on the sciatic nerve when it shortens. That's the ache that wraps from your hip into your leg after a long sit.

Lie on your back, both knees bent, feet flat. Cross your right ankle over your left knee. Thread your hands behind your left thigh and pull that knee toward your chest. When you feel the stretch deep in the right glute, hold for 60 seconds. Breathe. Switch sides. Do this every evening and you'll catch the pattern before it sets.

### 2. A tool — a frozen water bottle ($0)

Fill a standard water bottle three-quarters full, freeze it overnight. Roll your bare foot over it for two minutes per side — slow, steady pressure from heel to ball of foot.

This is the best thing I know of for feet that have been standing on hot concrete, walking through fairs, or just absorbing summer. The cold reduces inflammation while the pressure releases the plantar fascia. It's free, it works, and the bottle goes back in the freezer when you're done. Do it while you're making dinner and it takes zero extra time.

### 3. A nugget — why your last massage might not have stuck

Here's something I explain in the treatment room more than anywhere else: if you book deep tissue when you actually need sports massage — or vice versa — the relief fades fast. They sound similar but they solve different problems. Deep tissue works through adhesions in chronically tight layers. Sports massage follows movement chains and targets the pattern that's creating the pain.

If your issue is tied to a specific motion — mowing, swinging, reaching overhead — sports massage logic usually gets further. If it's a deep, dull ache that's been there for weeks regardless of what you do, deep tissue is probably the call. Knowing the difference before you book saves you time and money.

### 4. A practice — the 2-minute jaw release

You're clenching right now. Most people are, and they don't know it until someone says it.

Place your fingertips on the muscles just in front of your ears, where your jaw hinges. Open your mouth slightly — about the width of two fingers. Press in gently with your fingertips and make small circles. Slow. Ten circles forward, ten circles back. Then let your jaw hang open for 30 seconds — mouth slightly ajar, tongue resting on the floor of the mouth, no effort.

Jaw tension feeds directly into neck tension, headaches, and shoulder tightness. Releasing it takes two minutes and changes the rest of the day. Do it at your desk, in the car (parked), or before bed.

### 5. A seasonal note

Full summer in Missouri. The long days feel good but they add up — more activity, more heat, more demand on a body that's been ramping since April. This is the time of year people come in and say "I don't know why I'm so tight, I haven't done anything." You have. You've been doing everything, for weeks, and your body kept a tab.

If you've been pushing and something's not letting go, come in. One session usually clears what rest alone won't. The table's open Monday, Wednesday, and Friday.
