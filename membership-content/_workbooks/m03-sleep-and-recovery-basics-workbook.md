## Sleep Environment Audit

Walk through your bedroom and check each item.

**Temperature:**
- Room temperature at sleep time: ____°F
- Target: 65-68°F
- Action needed: [ ] None / [ ] Lower thermostat / [ ] Open window / [ ] Fan

**Light:**
- Room dark enough? [ ] Pitch black / [ ] Mostly dark / [ ] Some ambient light / [ ] Bright
- Action needed: [ ] None / [ ] Blackout curtains / [ ] Sleep mask / [ ] Cover light sources

**Sound:**
- Consistent or variable? [ ] Quiet / [ ] Consistent (white noise, fan) / [ ] Variable (traffic, people)
- Action needed: [ ] None / [ ] White noise / [ ] Earplugs / [ ] Address source

**Pillow check:** Lie on your side. Is your head in line with your spine?
- [ ] Yes — pillow is right
- [ ] Head tips DOWN — pillow too thin (add another or replace)
- [ ] Head tips UP — pillow too thick (replace or fold over)

**Mattress check:** Lie flat on your back. Can you slide your hand under your low back?
- [ ] Slides in snugly — mattress is right
- [ ] Slides through with room to wiggle — mattress too firm (add topper)
- [ ] Can't fit hand at all — mattress too soft (replace, or use firmer surface)

**Sleep position:** What's your usual?
- [ ] Side with pillow between knees (best)
- [ ] Side without pillow between knees
- [ ] Back with pillow under knees
- [ ] Back flat
- [ ] Stomach (worst — consider switching)

## Try this for 7 days

If you do nothing else from this workbook, try this:

That's the experiment.

Most people who actually do this for 7 days are shocked at the difference by day 5. The most reliable sleep improvement intervention I know.
