# The Year of Your Mid-Back — Companion Workbook

*A year-long companion to the IC3 deep cut. Map your mid-back's patterns and track your progress.*

---

## How to use this workbook

Year-long reference. Pattern self-assessment when something flares. 30-day tracker when starting new practice. Seasonal check-ins.

Bring it to every session.

## Pattern Self-Assessment

### Mobility Self-Tests (do these before answering questions)

**Test 1 — Rotation.** Stand. Cross arms over chest. Without moving hips, rotate slowly to right as far as comfortable. Can you see directly behind you?

- [ ] Yes, fully
- [ ] Halfway behind
- [ ] Just over my shoulder
- [ ] Barely past my front shoulder

Switch to left. [ ] Same / [ ] Less / [ ] More than right

**Test 2 — Extension.** Stand tall. Hands on hips. Gently arch backward. Where do you feel the bend?

- [ ] Mid-back (between shoulder blades)
- [ ] Low back only
- [ ] Neck only
- [ ] Don't feel any bend

**Test 3 — Breathing.** Place one hand on chest, one on belly. Take a deep breath without changing anything else.

- [ ] Only belly moves (healthy)
- [ ] Both chest and belly
- [ ] Mostly chest
- [ ] Shoulders rise with breath

### Right now, my mid-back feels:

**Pain level (0-10):** [____]

**Where exactly?**

- [ ] Both sides equally
- [ ] One side only — [ ] Right / [ ] Left
- [ ] Specific localized spot
- [ ] Wraps around to front (could indicate intercostal or shingles — see doctor)

## Try this for 30 days

If you do nothing else from this workbook, try this:

**Open-book stretch. 5 reps each side. Twice a day. Every day for 30 days.**

That's 4 minutes a day total. Two short sessions.

The mid-back is the area that responds fastest to consistent attention. Most people see real, structural change in 30 days with this one stretch alone — better breathing, easier posture, less neck and shoulder tension as downstream benefits.

Bring this workbook to your next session.
