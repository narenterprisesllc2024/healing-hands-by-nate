# Mid-Back and Rib Cage — Companion Workbook

*A printable companion to the Month 8 module. Fill it in as you go.*

## How to use this workbook

Print it. Keep it where you'll do the work — bedroom floor, living room, whatever space you stretch in.

The mid-back is the area that responds fastest to consistent attention. This workbook is built to help you notice the changes as they happen, because they happen quickly and you'll forget how stuck you used to feel.

Bring it to your next session.

---

## Where You Are Today

**Do a quick mobility self-test before starting anything.** Stand up. Feet shoulder-width apart. Hands crossed over your chest.

Without moving your hips, **rotate your upper body slowly to the right** as far as you comfortably can. Note where you end up — can you see directly behind you? Halfway behind you?

Now rotate to the left.

Now stand normally. Try to **arch backward gently** — chin up, chest forward, shoulders back. Where do you feel the bend? In your mid-back, or only in your low back and neck?

**Record:**

- Rotation to the right: I can see [ ] my front shoulder / [ ] over my shoulder / [ ] partway behind me / [ ] fully behind me

## Try this for 7 days

If you do nothing else from this workbook, try this:

**Open-book stretch. 5 reps each side. Once in the morning, once before bed. Every day for one week.**

That's the assignment. Two minutes a day. Two times a day.

The mid-back is the area that responds fastest to consistent attention. Most people see real change in a week with this one move alone. If you only do one thing, do this one.

Bring this workbook to your next session. I want to see what your rotation looks like after a week.
