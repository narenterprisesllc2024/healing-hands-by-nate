## Sleep Environment Audit

Walk through your bedroom and assess each item honestly.

### Temperature

- Current room temperature at bedtime: ____°F
- Target: 65-68°F
- Action needed: [ ] None / [ ] Lower thermostat / [ ] Fan / [ ] Open window

### Light

- Room darkness: [ ] Pitch black / [ ] Mostly dark / [ ] Some ambient / [ ] Bright
- Action needed: [ ] None / [ ] Blackout curtains / [ ] Sleep mask / [ ] Cover light sources / [ ] Remove devices with displays

### Sound

- Consistency: [ ] Quiet / [ ] Consistent (white noise/fan) / [ ] Variable / [ ] Loud
- Action needed: [ ] None / [ ] White noise machine / [ ] Earplugs / [ ] Address source

### Pillow check

Lie on your side. Is your head in line with your spine?

- [ ] Yes — pillow is right
- [ ] Head tips DOWN — pillow too thin (add another or replace)
- [ ] Head tips UP — pillow too thick (replace or fold over)

### Mattress check

Lie flat on your back. Slide your hand under your low back.

- [ ] Slides snugly — mattress is right
- [ ] Slides with room to wiggle — too firm (add topper)
- [ ] Can't fit hand — too soft (replace or use firmer surface)

### Sleep position

- [ ] Side with pillow between knees (best)
- [ ] Side without pillow between knees
- [ ] Back with pillow under knees
- [ ] Back flat
- [ ] Stomach (consider switching)

### Bed/bedroom use

- [ ] Bed is for sleep and sex only
- [ ] Sometimes work or watch TV in bed
- [ ] Often work, watch TV, scroll in bed

## Try this for 30 days

If you do nothing else from this workbook, try this:

**Phone in another room. Bed by 10:30 PM. Wake at the same time daily, including weekends. Caffeine cut off by noon. 30 days.**

Don't add anything else. Just these four things.

Most people who actually do this for 30 days are shocked at how different they feel. This is the highest-leverage sleep intervention.

Bring this workbook to your next session.
