## Warning Signs Quick-Reference (cut out and keep with your tracker)

**See your doctor if any of these show up after mowing:**

- Sharp, shooting pain down one leg
- Numbness or tingling in legs or feet
- Weakness in legs (foot drop, can't push off properly)
- Pain that doesn't improve at all over 5-7 days
- Pain that gets worse instead of better
- Sudden severe pain during activity (something popped or tore)

**Go to the ER immediately for:**

- Bladder or bowel function changes
- Loss of sensation in the saddle area (between the legs)

Normal mowing soreness doesn't include any of the above. If you have these, don't try to stretch through it.

## Try this for 7 days

If you do nothing else from this workbook, try this:

**Glute squeezes. 10 reps. Three times a day. Every day for one week. Pre-season.**

Stand, sit, walking — doesn't matter. Just squeeze your glutes hard for 5 seconds, release, repeat 10 times. Morning, midday, evening.

This wakes up the muscles that should be doing the work when you mow. Most people are stunned by how weak their glutes are after winter. A week of activation makes the May low back lockup dramatically less likely.
