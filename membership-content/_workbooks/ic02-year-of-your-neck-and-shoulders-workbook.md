Use this when neck or shoulder pain shows up.

- Neck: [____]
- Right shoulder: [____]
- Left shoulder: [____]
- Headaches: [____]

**Where exactly?**

- [ ] Both upper traps, both sides of neck
- [ ] One-sided neck and shoulder — [ ] Right / [ ] Left
- [ ] Deep in the shoulder joint
- [ ] Base of skull, into temples (headaches)
- [ ] Down the arm / into the hand (SEE DOCTOR)

### Pattern questions

**1. When does it hurt most?**

- [ ] Progressively through the workday → Pattern 1 (Desk Worker Hunch)
- [ ] During stressful periods → Pattern 2 (Tension Storage)
- [ ] Mornings, when waking → Pattern 3 (Sleep Position Damage)
- [ ] Specific movements (overhead, behind back) → Pattern 4 (Rotator Cuff)
- [ ] All the time, mostly stiff, getting worse over weeks → Pattern 5 (Frozen Shoulder)

**2. Have you lost range of motion?**

- [ ] No, just pain
- [ ] Yes, progressively over weeks (POSSIBLE FROZEN SHOULDER — see doctor)
- [ ] Yes, but only with one specific movement (overhead reaching)

**3. Does it refer down the arm?**

- [ ] Stays in neck/shoulder
- [ ] Numbness or tingling into the arm or hand (SEE DOCTOR)
- [ ] Refers into headache pattern

## Try this for 30 days

If you do nothing else from this workbook, try this:

**Doorframe stretch. Three times a day. 30 seconds each. Every day for 30 days.**

That's 90 seconds a day total. Three brief visits to any doorframe in your house.

If your neck and shoulders have been chronically tight and you do just this one stretch consistently for 30 days, the change is often dramatic. Most people see real difference by day 10-14.

Bring this workbook to your next session.
