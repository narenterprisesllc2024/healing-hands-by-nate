# Breath as Regulation — Companion Workbook

*A printable companion to the Month 2 module. Build the breath practice that retrains your nervous system.*

---

## How to use this workbook

Print it. Put it near where you'll do the 5-minute daily practice — bedside table, coffee maker, desk.

This is a habit-building workbook. The breath practice itself takes 5 minutes. The workbook keeps you honest about whether you're actually doing it.

Bring it to your next session.

## Where You Are Today — Breath Pattern Self-Assessment

**Test 1 — Sitting watch:** Sit normally. Watch yourself breathe for 30 seconds without changing anything. What moves?

- [ ] Shoulders rising and falling (shoulder breathing)
- [ ] Upper chest expanding (chest breathing)
- [ ] Belly rising and falling (diaphragmatic breathing)
- [ ] Belly + chest both moving (mixed)

**Test 2 — Lying watch:** Lie flat on your back. Hand on chest, hand on belly. Breathe normally for one minute. Which hand moves more?

- [ ] Chest hand significantly more
- [ ] Chest hand slightly more
- [ ] About equal
- [ ] Belly hand slightly more
- [ ] Belly hand significantly more

**Test 3 — Count test:** How long is your natural inhale? Count: __ counts. How long is your natural exhale? Count: __ counts.

## Try this for 7 days

If you do nothing else from this workbook, try this:

**Ten slow breaths every night before bed. Inhale 4, exhale 6. For one week.**

Just before you turn out the light. Doesn't have to be on the floor, doesn't have to be elaborate. Sitting on the edge of the bed works. Lying down works.

Ten breaths. Slow. Belly moves, shoulders don't.

Most people fall asleep faster within 4-5 nights. That's the whole experiment.
