# Hips and Hip Flexors — Companion Workbook

*A printable companion to the Month 9 module. Fill it in as you go.*

---

## How to use this workbook

Print it. Keep it where you'll do the work — bedroom floor, living room rug, wherever you'll actually stretch.

The hips are slow-changing but durable. Once you make progress, it sticks — unlike the mid-back or jaw, which need ongoing attention to hold. This workbook is built to help you notice the changes as they happen and connect them to the downstream stuff that's been bothering you.

Bring it to your next session.

---

## Where You Are Today

**Do these three quick tests before starting anything.**

**Test 1 — Hip flexor length.** Lie flat on your back on the floor. Pull your right knee to your chest. While holding the right knee, let your left leg lie flat on the floor.

Does your left leg stay flat? Or does it lift off the floor when you pull the right knee in?

- [ ] Stays flat against the floor (good hip flexor length)
- [ ] Lifts slightly (some tightness)
- [ ] Lifts noticeably (significant tightness)
- [ ] Can't even pull the right knee fully to chest (severe tightness)

Switch sides and check the right hip flexor:

- [ ] Stays flat / [ ] Lifts slightly / [ ] Lifts noticeably / [ ] Can't fully bend

**Test 2 — Couch stretch reaction.** Try the couch stretch from the module. After 30 seconds, rate:

- Right side intensity (0-10): [____]
- Left side intensity (0-10): [____]

**Test 3 — 90-90 position.** Can you get into the 90-90 sit?

- [ ] Yes, comfortably
- [ ] Yes, but it's tight
- [ ] Sort of — need to sit on a pillow
- [ ] Can't — I tip backward or it's painful

**Rate each symptom (0 = none, 10 = severe):**

```
Low back tightness when standing up after sitting [____]
Knee pain or stiffness                            [____]
"Stiff" feeling first thing in the morning        [____]
Trouble sitting cross-legged                      [____]
Walking gait feels "stiff"                        [____]
Hip pain in the front when lifting your knee      [____]
```

## Try this for 7 days

If you do nothing else from this workbook, try this:

**Couch stretch. 30 seconds each side. Twice a day. Every day for one week.**

That's the assignment. Two minutes a day, total.

If your low back has been bothering you for years and you do this for seven days, you'll feel a difference. If you don't, message me and we'll figure out what else is going on.
