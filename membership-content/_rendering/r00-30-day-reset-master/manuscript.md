**30 days. 2-3 minutes per day. One practice per day.**

That's it. Less time per day than checking your phone.

You don't have to do it perfectly. You don't have to be flexible already. You don't have to have time. You have to commit to showing up for 2-3 minutes a day for 30 days.

**Week 1 — Breath & Awareness:** The foundation. Learning to read your own body. Establishing the breath as your reset button.

**Week 2 — Mid-Back & Neck:** The upper body that desk work, phones, and modern life have locked up. Restoration of mobility.

### How to use this program

**Read one day's entry each morning or whenever you'll do the practice.** Each day is short — 2-3 minutes to read, 2-3 minutes to do. Total: under 6 minutes per day.

**Do the practice that day.** Just that day's practice. Don't try to do all of them. Don't get ahead.

**Trust the sequence.** Each week builds on the previous one. The order matters. Don't skip ahead.

# WEEK 1 — Breath & Awareness

*The foundation. You can't change patterns you can't see. This week we develop the awareness that makes everything else possible.*

---

## Day 1 — Notice Your Breath

Your first assignment is the simplest one: just watch yourself breathe for two minutes without changing anything.

**The practice:**

- Sit or lie down comfortably
- Set a timer for 2 minutes
- Just watch your breath
- Notice where the movement is — chest, belly, shoulders, all three
- Don't change anything. Don't try to "breathe better." Just observe
- Count your natural breath rate for the last 60 seconds — how many breaths in a minute

**Why this matters:**

Most people don't know how they breathe. They've never paid attention. This is the foundation everything else builds on — if you don't notice what your body is doing, you can't change it. Today is observation only.

**Closing thought:**

That's it. Just noticing. Most people are surprised by what they find when they actually look. We'll build from here tomorrow.

---

## Day 2 — Belly Breathing 101

Yesterday you watched. Today you shift.

**The practice:**

- Lie down on your back
- Place one hand on your chest, one on your belly
- Inhale slowly through your nose for 4 counts
- Exhale slowly through your mouth for 6 counts
- ONLY the belly hand should move. Chest hand stays still.
- Do 10 breaths slowly

**Why this matters:**

Diaphragmatic breathing — belly-dominant breathing — activates your parasympathetic nervous system. The longer exhale is the key. Shoulder breathing activates stress. Belly breathing activates recovery.

If you noticed yesterday that your shoulders rise with each breath, that's stress breathing. This is the reset.

**Closing thought:**

Ten breaths. Do them again before bed tonight. Two sets of ten breaths today. That's your day.

---

## Day 3 — The 60-Second Body Scan

Now we add awareness of the whole body.

**The practice:**

- Sit or stand
- Close your eyes
- Move your attention slowly from head to feet
- At each station, just notice — am I tense here? Can I let it go?
- Stations: jaw, forehead, neck, shoulders, chest, hands, stomach, hips, legs, feet

**Why this matters:**

Most people carry tension they don't know about. Once you notice it, it often releases on its own. The scan trains the awareness that makes self-care possible.

**Closing thought:**

60 seconds. Do it three times today — morning, midday, evening. You'll be surprised what you find when you actually look.

---

## Day 4 — Breathing Into Tension

Combine yesterday and today.

**The practice:**

- Do the body scan from Day 3
- Find the tightest area
- Now do 5 slow breaths, directing each inhale toward that tight area
- Imagine the breath filling that spot
- Long slow exhale, letting the area soften

**Why this matters:**

Breath goes where attention goes. This sounds metaphorical. It's not — it's how your nervous system works. Combining breath with attention to specific areas is one of the most effective body-based tools you have.

**Closing thought:**

Two minutes. Take any tight area you find through this practice today. Anywhere, anytime.

---

## Day 5 — The Exhale Reset

The exhale is the reset button. Today we lengthen it deliberately.

**The practice:**

- Sit or lie comfortably
- Inhale 4 counts through your nose
- Exhale 8 counts through your mouth (if 8 is too long, do 6)
- Do 8 breaths
- Notice what happens to your shoulders, your jaw, your stomach

**Why this matters:**

The exhale phase activates the parasympathetic nervous system more strongly than the inhale. Longer exhales = stronger relaxation signal. This is a portable reset you can use anywhere — driving, in a meeting, before bed.

**Closing thought:**

Eight breaths with a long exhale. That's your reset button. You can hit it anywhere, anytime, and nobody will know.

---

## Day 6 — Morning Breath Practice

Build the habit. 5 minutes of belly breathing before you get out of bed tomorrow morning.

**The practice (do this in the morning):**

- Before sitting up, stay lying down
- Hands on belly
- Slow nasal inhale, 4 counts
- Slow mouth exhale, 6 counts
- Continue for 5 minutes (set a timer if needed)

**Why this matters:**

Five minutes of breathing first thing sets your nervous system tone for the day. It's the cheapest intervention with the biggest return. People who do this for 30 days notice their daytime stress level drops without doing anything else.

**Closing thought:**

Five minutes before the day starts. This is the foundation. Everything we do for the next 24 days builds on this.

# WEEK 2 — Mid-Back & Neck

*The upper body that desk work, phones, and modern life have locked up. This week we restore mobility.*

---

## Day 8 — Cat-Cow for the Mid-Back

Wake up the thoracic spine.

**The practice:**

- Hands and knees on floor
- Wrists under shoulders, knees under hips
- INHALE: drop belly, lift head, look up (cow position)
- EXHALE: round back toward ceiling, tuck chin (cat position)
- 10 slow reps
- Focus on getting your MID-BACK to move, not just your low back and neck

**Why this matters:**

Most adults' mid-back has been locked in forward-rounded position for years. Cat-cow is the gentle re-introduction of motion. Pay attention to the area between your shoulder blades — that's where most people are stuck.

**Closing thought:**

Ten reps. Slow. If you felt crackling or popping, that's normal — that's your spine saying thanks for the attention.

---

## Day 9 — Neck Circles and Tilts

Gentle neck mobility in every direction.

**The practice:**

- Sitting or standing tall
- Slow neck circles: 5 in each direction
- Ear to shoulder tilt: hold 15 seconds each side
- Chin to chest: hold 15 seconds (do NOT crank head backward)

**Why this matters:**

Your neck has been locked in one position all day, every day. Give it the full range of motion at least once daily.

**Closing thought:**

Slow. Always slow with the neck. Your neck has been locked in one position all day. Give it the full range of motion once. That's all it's asking for.

---

## Day 10 — The Doorframe Stretch

The most important stretch for anyone with a desk, a steering wheel, or a phone.

**The practice:**

- Find a doorframe
- Stand in it, forearms on the frame at shoulder height, elbows at 90 degrees
- Step one foot forward through the doorway
- Lean your chest forward until you feel the stretch across your chest
- Hold 30 seconds. Breathe.
- Repeat 3 times.

**Why this matters:**

Hours of arms-forward posture (typing, driving, phone) shortens your chest muscles, pulling shoulders forward. Doorframe stretch is the only reliable counter to this. If you do this consistently, your neck and shoulder tension will drop noticeably within two weeks.

**Closing thought:**

Three times, 30 seconds each. Do this every single day and your neck pain will drop by half. I'm not exaggerating.

---

## Day 11 — Thoracic Extension Over a Roller

Open the mid-back with a foam roller or rolled-up towel.

**The practice:**

- Lie back over a foam roller (or tightly rolled bath towel) placed at mid-back level, perpendicular to your spine
- Arms behind your head supporting your neck
- Gentle extension over the roller — let your upper body arch backward over it
- Hold 30 seconds at each position
- Roll the roller up or down on your spine and repeat
- Work through 3-5 positions along the mid-back

**Why this matters:**

This addresses what cat-cow can't reach. The mid-back has specific segments that need direct attention. The roller does what your own movements can't.

**Closing thought:**

That stiff spot in your mid-back — the one that felt like it hadn't moved in a year — that's what's been driving your neck and shoulder problems. Keep opening it.

---

## Day 12 — Upper Trap Release

Self-release the upper trapezius — the tight muscle between your neck and shoulder.

**The practice:**

- Cross your right arm over your body and grab your left upper trap (the meaty muscle between your neck and shoulder)
- Squeeze the muscle firmly while slowly tilting your head AWAY from that side
- Hold 20 seconds
- Switch sides
- Or: use a lacrosse ball against a wall if your hand grip isn't strong enough

**Why this matters:**

This area accumulates stress and posture tension daily. Direct self-release works. Don't wait for a massage to address it.

**Closing thought:**

Both sides, 20 seconds each. Do this every time you feel your shoulders creeping up toward your ears.

---

## Day 13 — Open-Book Stretch

The mobility drill that opens your mid-back AND your chest in one move.

**The practice:**

- Lie on your side
- Knees stacked at 90 degrees (hips bent 90, knees bent 90)
- Both arms straight out in front of you, palms together
- Slowly rotate your top arm in an arc over your body, like turning a book page
- Follow it with your eyes
- Let your chest rotate open — don't force your top shoulder to the floor
- Hold the end position for 2-3 breaths
- Return. Repeat 5 times each side.

**Why this matters:**

This is the single most effective thoracic mobility exercise I know. If you keep one thing from this whole program, keep this. Five reps each side, twice a day, and your mid-back, breathing, and shoulders will all change within two weeks.

**Closing thought:**

This is the one you're going to want to do forever. Five each side, every day. Your mid-back and your breathing will both get better.

# WEEK 3 — Hips & Low Back

*The lower body that sitting has shortened. This week we release the patterns causing most chronic back pain.*

---

## Day 15 — Hip Circles

Get the hip joints moving through their full range.

**The practice:**

- Stand on one leg (hold a wall for balance if needed)
- Circle the other hip in big, slow circles
- 10 circles in each direction
- Switch legs and repeat

**Why this matters:**

Your hips are ball-and-socket joints. They're supposed to move in circles. Most adults haven't moved their hips in circles in years. This is the simple re-introduction of normal hip motion.

**Closing thought:**

Twenty circles each hip. That's two minutes. Your hips just got more range of motion than they've had in months.

---

## Day 16 — The Couch Stretch

The best hip flexor stretch you're not doing.

**The practice:**

- Find a couch or wall
- Kneel facing AWAY from the couch
- Place your right foot UP on the couch behind you, shin vertical against the back of the couch
- Right knee on the floor near the base of the couch
- Left foot flat on the floor in front of you, knee at 90 degrees
- Stand tall — chest up, shoulders back
- Squeeze your right glute. Push your right hip slightly forward.
- Hold 30 seconds
- Switch sides

**Why this matters:**

This stretches the deep hip flexors (psoas) that get chronically shortened by sitting. The intensity is real. If this is brutal, that's your answer for why your low back hurts. Don't bail — hold it.

**Closing thought:**

Thirty seconds each side. If this was brutal, that's your answer for why your low back has been bothering you.

---

## Day 17 — Knees to Chest and Low Back Release

Decompress the lumbar spine.

**The practice:**

- Lie on your back
- Pull both knees to your chest, gently
- Rock side to side slowly for 30 seconds
- Then: extend one leg flat, hold the other knee to chest, 20 seconds each side
- Finish with: gentle spinal twist — both knees to one side, arms out in a T, hold 30 seconds each side

**Why this matters:**

The low back compresses all day from sitting and standing. This sequence decompresses it directly. Use this any day your low back is "talking to you."

**Closing thought:**

This is your go-to for any day your low back is bothering you. Takes two minutes. Do it on the floor, not the bed — the firm surface matters.

---

## Day 18 — The 90-90 Hip Position

Open the hips in rotation.

**The practice:**

- Sit on the floor
- Front leg at 90 degrees in front of you, foot pointing to the left
- Back leg at 90 degrees behind you, foot pointing to the back
- Both knees and hips form 90-degree angles
- Sit tall — don't lean
- Breathe
- Hold 30 seconds, switch sides
- If you can't get to the floor comfortably, sit on a pillow

**Why this matters:**

This position tells you immediately how tight your hips are. Some people can't get into it at all. That's data, not failure. Sit on a pillow if needed. The position itself is the work.

**Closing thought:**

Wherever you are today is where you are. No judgment. Do this every day and you'll be somewhere different in two weeks.

---

## Day 19 — Glute Bridge

Wake up the muscles that should be supporting your low back.

**The practice:**

- Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat on the floor
- Squeeze your glutes and lift your hips toward the ceiling
- Hold at the top for 3 seconds
- 15 reps, slow

**If your hamstrings cramp:** That's important information. It means your glutes aren't firing properly — your hamstrings are doing the work the glutes should be doing. That's the whole point of this exercise. Keep doing them. The glutes will wake up over time.

**Why this matters:**

Weak glutes are one of the main causes of chronic low back pain. The low back compensates for weak glutes. Strong glutes = relaxed low back.

**Closing thought:**

Fifteen reps, slow. If your hamstrings did all the work, that tells you everything about why your low back has been bothering you.

---

## Day 20 — Child's Pose and Low Back Breathing

Combine stretch with breath for a deep low back release.

**The practice:**

- On hands and knees
- Spread your knees wide
- Sit back toward your heels
- Reach your arms forward on the floor
- Lower your chest toward the floor
- Breathe INTO your low back — feel it expand on each inhale
- Hold for 2 minutes

**Why this matters:**

The combination of the stretch position and directed breath is more powerful than either alone. This is one of the most relaxing releases you can do for your low back.

**Closing thought:**

Two minutes in child's pose, breathing into your low back. That's more therapy than most people's bodies get in a month.

# WEEK 4 — Sleep & Wind-Down

*The recovery practice that makes everything else stick. Body-based sleep optimization.*

---

## Day 22 — Legs Up the Wall

The simplest nervous system reset there is.

**The practice:**

- Lie on your back next to a wall
- Swing your legs up the wall, getting your butt as close to the wall as comfortable
- Arms at your sides or on your belly
- Close your eyes
- Breathe normally
- Stay 3-5 minutes

**Why this matters:**

The inverted leg position drains pooled blood from your legs back toward your heart, activating the parasympathetic response. This is one of the fastest, most reliable nervous-system resets there is. Looks like nothing. Does a lot.

**Closing thought:**

This looks like nothing. It's everything. Five minutes legs up the wall before bed will reset your nervous system faster than anything else in this program.

---

## Day 23 — Progressive Muscle Relaxation

Tighten and release every muscle group, one at a time.

**The practice:**

- Lying down, eyes closed
- Start with feet: curl toes HARD for 10 seconds, then release completely for 10
- Then calves: flex feet upward, hold 10, release 10
- Continue up: thighs, glutes, stomach, fists, shoulders, face
- 10 seconds tight, 10 seconds released
- Notice the difference between tense and relaxed at each station

**Why this matters:**

This teaches your body what "relaxed" actually feels like, by contrast with "tense." Many adults have lost the ability to fully relax muscles because they don't know what relaxed feels like anymore. This rebuilds that awareness.

**Closing thought:**

That feeling right now — heavy, loose, warm — that's what your body is supposed to feel like before sleep. Now you know how to get there.

---

## Day 24 — The Spinal Twist Before Bed

Decompress the spine and calm the nervous system.

**The practice:**

- Lie on your back
- Arms out in a T position
- Both knees fall to one side, look in the opposite direction
- Hold 1-2 minutes
- Switch sides

**Why this matters:**

A gentle supine twist decompresses the spine after the day's compression and signals your nervous system to wind down. Combine with slow breathing for maximum effect.

**Closing thought:**

Both sides. Two minutes each. This is in your bedtime routine now. Don't argue with me — just do it.

---

## Day 25 — Jaw Release Before Bed

Undo the day's clenching so you don't carry it into your sleep.

**The practice:**

- Sitting or lying
- Place fingertips on the masseter muscle (just below your cheekbone)
- Small slow circles, 30 seconds each side
- Open mouth wide, shift jaw left and right gently
- Settle into rest position: lips together, teeth apart, tongue on roof of mouth
- Three deep breaths in that position

**Why this matters:**

Most people clench their jaw at night. Releasing it before bed reduces the grinding and clenching, which reduces morning soreness and tension headaches.

**Closing thought:**

Lips together, teeth apart, tongue on the roof of your mouth. That's your jaw's resting position. Go to sleep that way.

---

## Day 26 — The Full Wind-Down Sequence

Combine everything from this week.

**The practice (10 minutes total):**

- Spinal twist, 1 minute each side (2 min)
- Legs up the wall, 3 minutes (3 min)
- Jaw release, 1 minute (1 min)
- Progressive muscle relaxation through whole body, 3 minutes (3 min)
- Diaphragmatic breathing, 1 minute (1 min)

**Why this matters:**

This sequence is what I've found works most reliably for transitioning from wakeful daily activity into sleep. Do this any night you're having trouble sleeping. The cumulative effect signals "we're done with the day, time to rest."

**Closing thought:**

Ten minutes before bed. That's the whole program. You now have a wind-down routine that'll serve you for the rest of your life.

---

## Day 27 — Sleep Environment Check

Quick audit of your sleep setup.

**The practice:**

Walk through your bedroom and check each:

- **Temperature:** 65-68°F is optimal
- **Light:** as dark as possible (blackout curtains or sleep mask)
- **Sound:** consistent (white noise is fine; variable noise is not)
- **Pillow:** appropriate to your sleep position
- **Bedding:** firm enough mattress, comfortable surface
- **Screens:** none in the bedroom ideally, or at minimum no screens 30 minutes before bed

**Why this matters:**

Your nervous system can do everything right but if the environment is fighting you, sleep quality suffers. Most sleep issues have an environmental component.

**Closing thought:**

Fix the room, fix the routine, fix the breathing. That's your sleep stack. Most of it costs nothing.
