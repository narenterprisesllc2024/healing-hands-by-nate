# End-of-Year Full-Body Inventory

*A Between Sessions bonus protocol for closing the year with a clear-eyed look at where you actually are.*

---

We're closing the year. I want you to take ten minutes and actually check in with your body — not how you think you feel, but how you actually feel, area by area.

Most people end the year on autopilot. December is busy. The body just keeps doing what it does. You arrive at January 1st with a vague sense of "another year" and no specific information about what your body actually needs in the coming year.

This inventory changes that. Ten minutes, once a year, to do an honest scan. Where do you hurt? Where are you stiff? Where did you make progress this year? What needs attention next year?

You don't need any equipment. Just this workbook, a pen, and 10 minutes of quiet.

## Why this matters

Without an end-of-year inventory, most people make resolutions that have nothing to do with what their body is actually telling them. "I should exercise more." "I should stretch more." "I should take better care of myself." Vague, untargeted, abandoned by February.

With an inventory, you have specific data. You know which three areas are loudest. You know what got better this year and what got worse. You know what to actually focus on in the new year — not what you think you should focus on, what your body is telling you to focus on.

That's the difference between January resolutions that fail and a body-care priority that actually holds through the year.

## The 10-minute inventory — area by area

For each area, do the same three steps:

1. **Move through its full range of motion** — slowly, with attention
2. **Rate pain right now** (0 = none, 10 = severe)
3. **Rate mobility right now** (0 = can't move it, 10 = full normal range)

Then take note: is this area better than January, worse than January, or about the same?

Now the most important step: name the three areas that need attention next year.

## Write it down where you'll see it

Take these three priorities and put them somewhere you'll see them in January:

- Bathroom mirror
- Bedroom wall
- Kitchen fridge
- Inside the cover of your planner
- Phone wallpaper

When January 1st comes around and you're tempted to make vague resolutions, you'll see your three actual priorities staring back at you. That's your real plan.

## When to come see me

If your inventory revealed an area that's been the same for a year (or worse), schedule a session in January. Don't wait for spring. The new year is when nervous-system pattern interruption works best — your body is more open to new inputs after the holiday reset.

If the inventory showed mostly improvement, congratulations. Maintenance is half the battle. Keep doing whatever you've been doing.

## Closing

Write down your three priorities. Put it somewhere you'll see it in January. That's your roadmap for next year. You know your body better than anyone — you just proved it.

Most people end the year without ever asking their body these questions. The bodies that get better over time are the bodies whose owners actually check in. You just did.
